Locations for Japan

Kyoto

Kyoto, Japan’s former imperial capital, is the heart of the nation’s history and culture. Home to thousands of temples, shrines, and gardens, it radiates timeless elegance and spiritual tranquility. Visitors can stroll through the ancient streets of Gion, where geisha traditions endure, or admire landmarks like Kinkaku-ji’s golden reflection shimmering over the lake. Each season paints Kyoto anew—cherry blossoms in spring, crimson leaves in autumn, and snow-covered temples in winter. Whether sipping matcha in a traditional teahouse or exploring bamboo groves in Arashiyama, Kyoto offers a deeply authentic journey into Japan’s artistic and cultural soul.

Tokyo

Tokyo is a dazzling blend of futuristic innovation and deep-rooted tradition, where neon skylines meet centuries-old temples. From the bustling streets of Shibuya and Shinjuku to the peaceful gardens of Meiji Shrine, the city offers endless contrasts and discoveries. Food lovers can savor everything from Michelin-star sushi to cozy ramen stalls, while tech enthusiasts explore Akihabara’s digital wonderland. In spring, cherry blossoms blanket Ueno Park, and in winter, Tokyo’s lights shimmer brighter than ever. Constantly evolving yet deeply respectful of its past, Tokyo captures the essence of modern Japan—a vibrant metropolis of creativity, culture, and unstoppable energy.

Places to visit in Kyoto

Nijo Castle

Nijo Castle (二条城, Nijōjō) was built in 1603 as the Kyoto residence of Tokugawa Ieyasu, the first shogun of the Edo Period (1603-1868). His grandson Iemitsu completed the castle's palace buildings 23 years later and expanded the castle by adding a five story castle keep.

Nishiki Market

Nishiki Market (錦市場, Nishiki Ichiba) is a narrow, five block long shopping street lined by more than one hundred shops and restaurants. Known as "Kyoto's Kitchen", this lively retail market specializes in all things food related, like fresh seafood, produce, knives and cookware, and is a great place to find seasonal foods and Kyoto specialties, such as Japanese sweets, pickles, dried seafood and sushi.

Places to visit in Tokyo

Mori Digital Art Museum

Highly popular, immersive digital art museum in Tokyo, known as the world's first museum of its kind. The museum features artworks without boundaries that move freely between rooms, interact with people, and create a continuous, ever-changing world.

Shibuya

Shibuya is the vibrant, energetic heart of modern Tokyo youth culture, renowned globally as the epicenter of fashion, entertainment, and nightlife. This district offers a unique blend of futuristic urban landscape and busy street life, making it a must-visit destination for those wanting to experience the pulse of contemporary Japan.
